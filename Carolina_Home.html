Crowdsourcing structured data about disaster, conflict and crime

Crowdsourcing is the process of obtaining information by enlisting the services of a large number of people.
Usually on a voluntary basis.
Structured data is information which has been given an internal logic which allows it to be easily categorized and processed.
Think of structured information like a spreadsheet with labeled rows and columns.
Whereas unstructured information is ‘free text’ – like this page.

Whenever a major natural disaster or conflict occurs, social media outlets are awash with unstructured information. This information is potentially of incredible value, providing insight into which geographical areas are effected, the nature and extent of the difficulties being experienced by the local population and specific details concerning individuals who require assistance. Unfortunately, sifting through this large volume of ‘free text’ is time consuming and inefficient, and aggregating it into a holistic overview of the situation in order to inform relief efforts is almost impossible.

That’s why this website exists.

It is a mechanism to allow people to submit key pieces of information (prompted by a set of questions on a form, to make sure that nothing is left out) in a structured way.

This structured data can be easily aggregated (combined with other similar data) and this allows us to:

  • Provide corroboration of events.
  • Identify patterns, trends and anomalies in the data.
  • View data on maps, graphs and charts in order to better understand complex situations.

T his data is then made available, via the map for analysis by disaster relief organizations, in order to help them provide the most efficient and effective response possible.
